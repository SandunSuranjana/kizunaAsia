Historical Timeline:

250 BC: Introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka by Arhat Mahinda.
Arhat Mahinda, son of Emperor Ashoka of India, arrives in Sri Lanka and introduces Buddhism to the island. This event marks a significant turning point in Sri Lankan history, shaping its cultural, religious, and social fabric.
3rd-5th Century AD: the Anuradhapura Kingdom flourishes.
The Anuradhapura Kingdom emerged as a powerful center of Theravada Buddhism. During this period, impressive temples, palaces, and irrigation systems were built, including the iconic Ruwanwelisaya stupa. Anuradhapura became a major pilgrimage site and a hub for trade and cultural exchange.
11th-13th Century AD: Polonnaruwa Kingdom thrives.

17th-18th Century AD: Dutch rule
In 1658 the Dutch captured Jaffna from the Portuguese, marking the beginning of their dominion over coastal regions. The Dutch captured the Sri Lankan coastal area from the Portuguese, further strengthening their presence in the region. They continue to expand trade, introduce new crops like cinnamon, and leave their mark on the island's architecture and legal system.

Historical Figures

King Dutugamunu

King Dutugamunu is revered as one of Sri Lanka's greatest heroes. He led a successful campaign to liberate Anuradhapura, the ancient capital, from the South Indian Chola invaders. This victory marked a pivotal moment in Sri Lankan history, consolidating power under the Anuradhapura Kingdom and uniting the country under a single ruler.
He is also known as the Patron of Buddhism. King Dutugamunu was a devout Buddhist and a generous patron of the religion. He built numerous temples and stupas, including the Ruwanwelisaya stupa, a magnificent monument that stands as a testament to his reign.
King Dutugamunu's legacy continues to inspire Sri Lankans today. He is remembered for his courage, leadership, and dedication to his country and religion. He is considered a symbol of national unity and strength

King Great Parakramabahu

King Parakramabahu the Great was another influential ruler of the Polonnaruwa Kingdom. He was a visionary leader who ushered in a golden age for Sri Lanka. One of his most significant achievements was the construction of numerous irrigation systems, including the Parakrama Samudra, a vast reservoir that still provides water to a large portion of the country today. These systems revolutionized agriculture and facilitated economic prosperity. King Parakramabahu was a staunch supporter of Theravada Buddhism. He built several temples and monasteries, patronized Buddhist scholars, and organized religious festivals. His efforts further solidified Buddhism's central role in Sri Lankan society. King Parakramabahu the Great is remembered as a wise and benevolent ruler who brought peace, stability, and prosperity to Sri Lanka. He is considered one of the greatest kings in Sri Lankan history, and his achievements continue to benefit the nation today.
